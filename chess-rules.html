♛ 4D Rhombus Chess — Rules

All chess rules currently applied in the game — with multi-level 3D movement

♟ Piece Movement (3D Multi-Level)
✓ IMPLEMENTED

♟ Pawn Movement (3D)

Same level: 1 square forward on X-axis. Captures diagonally on Z-axis.
Cross-level: Both white and black can move to either adjacent level (L+1 or L-1), keeping the same X/Z position. Can also capture diagonally on either adjacent level.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♟ Pawn Double Move

On their first move, pawns can advance 2 squares forward instead of 1 (on the same level only), as long as both squares are empty. Starting position: row 1 (white) or row 6 (black).

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♜ Rook Movement (3D)

Same level: Slides any distance along X-axis or Z-axis (4 directions).
Cross-level: Slides straight up or down through levels (2 directions). 6 total directions. Cannot jump over pieces.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♝ Bishop Movement (3D)

Same level: 4 diagonals (X+Z, X-Z, -X+Z, -X-Z).
Cross-level: 4 diagonals through levels (L+X, L-X, -L+X, -L-X). 8 total directions. Cannot jump over pieces.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♞ Knight Movement (3D)

Same level: 8 classic L-shaped jumps (2+1 pattern).
Cross-level: 8 diagonal jumps — 1 level ± 1 X ± 1 Z (all 8 combinations). 16 total moves. Can jump over pieces.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♛ Queen Movement (3D)

Same level: 4 straight + 4 diagonals (like rook + bishop).
Cross-level: Straight up/down (L), plus L+X, L-X, L+Z, L-Z diagonals. 18 total directions. Most powerful piece.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♚ King Movement (3D)

All directions: 1 square in any of the 26 adjacent cells — including diagonals and cross-level moves. Moves in a 3×3×3 cube around its position (minus itself).

⚔️ Captures & Special Rules
✓ IMPLEMENTED

⚔️ Captures Across Levels

Same level: Any piece captures by moving to an enemy's square on the same board.
Cross-level: Pieces that can move to another level can also capture there — a rook sliding up, a bishop sliding diagonally, a knight jumping, etc. Captures work identically across all 15 levels.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

♛ Pawn Promotion

A pawn promotes when it reaches column 7 (white) or column 0 (black) — the last column of the center level — regardless of which level the pawn is on. Works the same in both 7-level and 15-level mode. Player chooses: Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight. AI auto-promotes to Queen.

✗ NOT IMPLEMENTED

👻 En Passant

When a pawn advances 2 squares and lands beside an enemy pawn, the enemy can capture it as if it only moved 1 square. Not implemented on any level — neither same-level nor cross-level.

👑 King & Check Rules (Multi-Level)
✓ IMPLEMENTED

⚡ Check Detection (3D)

The game scans every piece on every level to detect if the king is under attack. A rook on level 3 can put a king on level 8 in check by sliding straight up. Works across all 7 or 15 levels. "CHECK!" warning appears and king flashes red.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

☠️ Checkmate (3D)

Checkmate is checked across all levels. The king must have zero escape moves on any level — same level, level above, or level below. An attacker from another level can deliver checkmate. Game ends with "CHECKMATE!"

✓ IMPLEMENTED

🤝 Stalemate (3D)

Stalemate is evaluated across all levels and all pieces. If a player has no legal moves on any level and is not in check, it's a draw. The extra levels give more escape routes, making stalemate harder to achieve than in standard chess.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

🛡️ Illegal Move Prevention

All moves are now validated before being shown or allowed. Any move that would leave your own king in check is filtered out — on any level. This applies to all pieces: moving away from protecting the king, moving the king into an attacked square, etc.

✗ NOT IMPLEMENTED

🏰 Castling

King moves 2 squares toward a rook, and the rook jumps over. Requires: neither piece has moved, no pieces between them, king not in/through check.

🎮 Game Rules
✓ IMPLEMENTED

🔄 Turn System

White always moves first. Turns alternate between white and black. You cannot move your opponent's pieces. Move counter tracks progression.

✓ IMPLEMENTED

🏗️ Multi-Level Board

15 (or 7) stacked levels in a rhombus shape. Middle level is widest (8×8), levels narrow toward top and bottom. All pieces can move between levels.

✗ NOT IMPLEMENTED

📏 50-Move Rule

In standard chess, if 50 moves pass with no captures or pawn moves, either player can claim a draw. Not tracked in the current game.

✗ NOT IMPLEMENTED

🔁 Threefold Repetition

If the same board position occurs 3 times, a draw can be claimed. No position tracking exists yet.
